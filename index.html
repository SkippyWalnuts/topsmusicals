About Us

Since 1927 we’ve put on over a hundred full productions, not to mention the many concerts and cabarets we perform in venues ranging from pubs and fêtes to the Royal Albert Hall and Twickenham Stadium. We've also been seen in films and television programmes, we've sung before royalty on the pitch at major England international rugby matches, and we've appeared in national touring productions of shows like Evita and Whistle Down the Wind for Bill Kenwright. We're also extremely proud of a significant number of our past members who have gone on to enjoy successful professional theatre careers and currently can be seen in national theatre tours, on cruise ships, on the West End stage and even on Broadway! We won the very first Swan Award for Best Musical Production when the Swans started in 1986 and since the year 2000 we have won the Swan Award for Best Musical no fewer than seven times, with six winners for Best Actress in a Musical, five Best Actor in a Musical, seven Best Supporting Actor/Actress in a Musical and one Best Young Performer. Gill Wade, Peter Holoway and Babette Langford have all won the Swan Accolade Award. We've also won numerous NODA awards over the years, including a number of the prestigious Flame Awards.

Our History

  • 1927 Founded by Tracey Arkell. Shows performed at York House
  • 1951 Peter Holloway joins as MD, later becoming Director and Chairman
  • 1960 Babette Langford becomes Choreographer
  • 1979 Gill Wade becomes Director
  • 1988 Peter Holloway retires and Mervyn-Bryn-Jones becomes MD. Move to Queen Charlotte Hall
  • 2000 Move to Richmond Theatre
  • 2002 Memorial plaque for Peter Holloway unveiled at Richmond Theatre
  • 2013 Gill Wade retires
  • 2014 Ian Stark becomes Chairman
  • 2015 Move to Hampton Hill Theatre
  • 2019 Flame Award for inspirational contribution to the pursuit of excellence in theatre

Management

  • CHAIR Ian Stark Ian has been a member of TOPS since 1969 when he performed opposite Bonnie Langford as one of the Snow Children in Carousel. Ian took over the chairmanship of TOPS in 2014.
  • VICE CHAIR Alan Charlton Alan joined TOPS in 1991 for Kiss Me Kate and although he moved out of the area he kept up his amateur stage career by joining his local society. He rejoined TOPS on moving back to the area and took over the role of Vice-Chair in 2014.
  • TREASURER Simon Fagan Simon's first show with TOPS was The Pirates of Penzance in 2013. He took over as Treasurer in 2022.
  • SECRETARY Fiona Stark Fiona met Ian when she joined TOPS for Guys & Dolls in 1989. Now she's his secretary!
